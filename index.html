    • ShaderToy

      Ray Tracing but in ShaderToy

      Following the books by Peter Shirley: Ray-Tracing in One Weekend and Ray-Tracing: The Next Week, I implemented a Ray-Tracer in ShaderToy. Also being a retry of the CS77 Assignment that was a real time ray-tracer that proved to be poor at rendering. The second book had me doing a lot of work arounds, abandoning my real-time ray-tracing attempt in favor of a static image render and constant resampling of the frames to create incredibly crisp images (Including the image that is featured on the top of this site). I ended up realizing that my code was poorly structured, and needed to fork a similar ray-tracer and reference it to rebuild my Ray-Tracer. Despite the spectular results, I learned that there is a lot for me to learn. I felt that this dive into Ray-Tracing was a stepping stone to go further into Computer Graphics.

    • ShaderToy

      CS77 Solar System Simulation

      Final Project for CS77 Winter 2020. This project was big for me in many ways. For one, it was the most successful group project I've ever had. Before I struggled in team projects, but after taking everything that I learned from those times, I worked together with my partner and friend, Jiro Mizuno, to create an impressive Solar System Simulation using ShaderToy. I was tasked with figuring out the process of using ShaderToy's limited work environment to simulate Kepler Orbital Mechanics and move objects. As well as book keeping, making sure that Jiro's work, being procedural texture generation, wouldn't interfere with my work. The final product speaks for itself, bugs and all. Even on our heavy time crunch then, we prevailed with an incredible simulation.
